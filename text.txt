# The Cognitive Supply Chain
## A Strategic Analysis of 50 Authors for Digital Intelligence Engines

---

## Executive Summary

The contemporary digital landscape has fundamentally shifted from information scarcity to attentional hyper-abundance. The primary currency is no longer raw data but *valence*—content that carries directed emotional, intellectual, or psychological energy.

This report analyzes 50 distinct authors and thinkers whose digital footprints represent the highest density of actionable insight across eight critical domains:

- **Productivity**
- **Dark Psychology**
- **Relationships**
- **Neuroscience**
- **Philosophy**
- **Healing**
- **Self-Care**
- **Self-Worth**

These domains form the fundamental axes upon which modern digital identity is constructed and contested.

---

## Methodology

### Selection Criteria

This analysis prioritized three key variables:

**1. Scrapeability & Structure**
Content exists in stable, text-heavy formats (WordPress, Substack, Medium) with clear taxonomies that enable systematic extraction and repurposing.

**2. Opinionated Valence**
Content resists commoditization by taking strong, often controversial stances on human nature, hierarchy, and biological reality. It generates engagement through directness rather than neutrality.

**3. Viral Utility**
Content is optimized to solve specific psychological problems or trigger high-arousal emotions, demonstrating clear utility to end users seeking transformation.

## Strategic Integration Framework

### Functional Content Layering

Content from these 50 authors operates on distinct semantic layers suitable for different engine applications:

| Layer | Function | Primary Authors | Engine Application |
|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Axiomatic** | Quotes, headlines, rules | Naval Ravikant, Derek Sivers, James Clear | Social media cards, push notifications, daily wisdom widgets |
| **Protocol** | Instructions, checklists, how-to | Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, Cal Newport | 30-day challenges, step-by-step guides, habit trackers |
| **Diagnostic** | Narratives, critiques, theory | Freddie deBoer, Mary Harrington, Mark Manson | Long-form newsletters, video scripts, deep dive audio |
| **Dark/Edge** | Controversy, reality checks, debate | Rollo Tomassi, Lucio Buffalmano, Illimitable Man | Viral hooks, community debate topics, reality testing |

### Content Journey Mapping

The engine should map citation networks to create meaningful content sequences:

**The Rationalist-Dark Triad Bridge:** Venkatesh Rao's analysis of corporate sociopathy connects directly to Lucio Buffalmano's social dominance analysis. Users interested in office politics benefit from content bridges.

**The Healing-Optimization Bridge:** Nicole LePera's nervous system regulation serves as the "software" counterpart to Peter Attia's longevity "hardware." Longevity content should include stress reduction content.

**The EvPsych-Culture Bridge:** Diana Fleischman's behavioral analysis explains dating market failures that Freya India critiques from cultural perspective.

## Conclusion

These 50 authors represent a "sovereign stack" of information—characterized by rejection of institutional narrative in favor of direct observation, biological realism, and systems thinking.

They offer the ideal mix of:
- **Evergreen utility** through rationalists and optimizers
- **High-valence engagement** through cultural critics and dark triad voices
- **Behavioral utility** through healing and somatic practitioners
- **Actionable frameworks** through protocol-based authors

Ingesting this corpus comprehensively maps the modern human condition—from neural firing to boardroom power dynamics. A content engine structured around these eight domains and six clusters can dynamically serve content that transforms user states: from anxiety to agency, confusion to clarity, passivity to power.
